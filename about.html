The creation of Loopify

During my running journey, I often found myself struggling to create diverse and interesting routes, especially when training for longer distances. I yearned for a tool that could effortlessly generate loop routes based on my desired distance, taking the guesswork out of route planning. This was the driving factor in creating Loopify. I wanted to create a solution that would not only help me but also benefit the entire running community. Loopify is the result of combining my passion for running with my skills in programming.

For those who are interested in the programming behind Loopify

First, it is important to understand that the data behind roads is represented as a graph. Where nodes represent turning points in a road, and edges are the line segment connecting the nodes. This is a good way to represent the information because it is easy to manipulate and store the data.

There are a number of algorithms and systems behind this Loopify. For example, rendering things without causing too much latency. To combat this issue, I made sure to only load objects such as bathrooms, traffic lights, and nodes & edges at a given zoom based on the users bounds.

Another would be the interaction with the map. After detecting map clicks, it needs to locate a point on a road (Road Snapping). To do this, I iterate through all the edges and find the start and end node. Then, you want to calculate where the point is closest to on an edge (node to node connection). To do this, you can use vector projection.
